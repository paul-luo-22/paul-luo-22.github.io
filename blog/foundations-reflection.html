Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I've actually reflected a lot over the years but how I think of myself seems very different from how others seem to form their self-identity. Things like culture or values don't really seem to weigh that heavily for me (I like/value kindness but I also like/value chocolate. Am I chocolate?). I think we have to be able to rationalise what we think of ourselves.

When I first heard about fixed mindset (during the intro video) I thought that would definitely be me (especially since that maths example is basically what I think of myself at maths). Surprisingly I have more growth mindset about some things than I'd thought.

I have a habit of tensing my jaw a lot unconciously that I didn't notice so much before having to do meditations.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Having empathy and being self-aware allows you to develop awareness of your own emotions, strengths and weaknesses which you can use to improve yourself or use to help you work well with others.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

There were a lot of times this part felt harder than the tech section.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Compared to the tech side a lot of the problem solving methods don't really work. It's not like you can google your values (although maybe google knows them better than you at this point). A lot of the time I'm trying to search for a right answer even though there is no right answer but the answer I have doesn't feel quite right either.
